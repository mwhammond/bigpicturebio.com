The Problem

Complex diseases don't fail
because of bad molecules. They fail because of a broken search.

Most therapeutics target a single mechanism in a single pathway. But diseases like Alzheimer's, treatment-resistant cancers, and autoimmune disorders are driven by networks of interacting mechanisms — different in every patient, different in every cell.

The result isn't just failure. It's systematic failure — and the combinatorial math makes brute-force screening impossible.

//

A 10-drug × 50-cell-type search space already exceeds what any lab can test experimentally. And real disease complexity is orders of magnitude larger.

Our Approach

Three pillars converge
into one engine.

We don't just apply AI to biology. We built a system where single-cell data, autonomous reasoning, and regulatory awareness feed each other — compressing the combinatorial explosion into a tractable, testable search.

01

Single-Cell Resolution

We map disease at the resolution of individual cells — capturing the heterogeneity that bulk assays miss. Every patient's disease is a mosaic; we read each tile.

scRNA-seq · spatial transcriptomics · cell-type deconvolution

02

Agentic AI

Autonomous agents reason over biological knowledge graphs, propose hypotheses, design experiments, and iteratively refine — compressing years of discovery into months.

hypothesis generation · knowledge graphs · iterative refinement

03

Regulatory Evolution

We design combination therapies with regulatory viability from day one — leveraging repurposed, approved drugs to accelerate the path from discovery to patients.

drug repurposing · approved compounds · de-risked paths

The Result

Rational Pruning

Instead of testing everything, we reason about what to test. Our engine prunes the combinatorial explosion of drug × cell-type × mechanism space into a focused set of biologically grounded, testable hypotheses.
